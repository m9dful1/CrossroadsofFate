## Path 1: Guard — "The Sentinel's Oath"

### Core Theme
Heroic epic with a Gnostic cosmological underpinning. The player begins as a righteous guard serving what they believe is a just order, only to gradually discover that the entire system — from the guard, to the city, to the temple, to the gods themselves — is a layered prison designed to keep divine sparks (Embers) trapped in darkness. The journey is one of *gnosis*: awakening to the true nature of reality.

**"As above, so below"** — each layer of worldly corruption mirrors the cosmic corruption. The corrupt captain mirrors the corrupt council mirrors the corrupt temple mirrors the Craftsman himself.

### Gnostic Cosmology

| Gnostic Term | In-Game Name | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Demiurge | **The Craftsman** | False creator god who built the material world as a prison |
| Archons | **The Architects** / **Archetypes** | Cosmic prison-keepers who maintain the false order |
| Pleroma | **The Fullness** | The true divine realm beyond the Craftsman's creation |
| Pneuma | **Embers** | Divine sparks of light trapped within mortal beings |
| Monad | **The Source** | The true, unknowable God beyond the Craftsman |
| Sophia | **Wisdom** | The divine quality that leads to gnosis (also a player stat!) |
| False Church | **The Order of the Architect** | The town's dominant religion; teaches obedience to the Craftsman's order |

**The Great Inversion:** What the Order calls "evil" — heretics, forbidden texts, the strange symbols — is actually the Source trying to awaken the Embers. What the Order calls "good" — obedience, hierarchy, the guard's mission — is actually the mechanism of imprisonment. But the Source works *through* apparent evil because the Craftsman controls all channels of apparent good.

### Tone
Heroic & epic — the player genuinely feels like a hero at the start. The world is vivid, the victories feel real. This makes the gradual unraveling all the more devastating and the final awakening all the more earned.

### Structure
**Case-based** — a series of investigations/missions that build on each other. Each case is self-contained but reveals pieces of the larger cosmic truth.

### Pacing
**Slow start, explosive end:**
- Layers 1-2 (Street + District) = ~50% of the storyline. Deep worldbuilding, establishing relationships, earning trust.
- Layer 3 (City Leadership) = the turning point. Everything begins to crack.
- Layers 4-5 (Religious Order + Cosmic Truth) = rapid, mind-bending climax.

### The Guard's Structure (Masonic Parallel)

**The Shield** — The public guard. Outer order. Regular ranks: Recruit → Watchman → Sergeant → Lieutenant → Captain. Members genuinely believe they serve justice.

**The Compass** — The secret inner order. Select members of the Shield are "elevated" (recruited) into The Compass. Members are called **Draftsmen** — those who "draft the blueprints" of the Craftsman's design. Each degree of initiation reveals more of the truth, binding the Draftsman deeper into complicity.

*"He's been drafted"* takes on a sinister double meaning.

The Compass mirrors the cosmic structure: degrees of apparent "illumination" that are actually degrees of imprisonment. The Draftsmen believe they serve a higher purpose, but they are the Architects' instruments on the mortal level.

### The Recurring Symbol

**The Keyhole with Light** — A keyhole shape with rays of light emanating from behind it. Found scratched into walls, hidden in old ruins, carved by secret gatherers. It predates the Order of the Architect by centuries.

- Represents: there is something beyond the locked door of reality. The key is gnosis.
- The Order calls it heresy — it implies their "perfect design" is a prison with an exit.
- Ties directly to the **holy_key** item already in the game.
- People who begin to awaken start drawing it instinctively, before they even understand why.

### Key NPCs

**Captain Aldric** — The player's mentor. Stern but fair, a decorated veteran who lives by the guard's code. Deeply devout to the Order of the Architect. Sees the player as his legacy. His tragedy: his faith is genuine. He's a good man serving a false cause.
- **Layer 1:** A rock of stability and guidance. Teaches the player the ropes.
- **Layer 2:** His dedication gets him noticed by The Compass. He's recruited ("drafted"). He starts acting strangely — more guarded, conflicted, absent.
- **Layer 2 climax:** Aldric is killed during a raid the player was supposed to join but was reassigned from at the last minute. The "criminals" involved have connections inside the guard. Officially: killed in the line of duty. The truth: he learned The Compass's secrets and couldn't live with them.
- **Legacy:** Investigating Aldric's death reveals a trail of clues leading into The Compass's operations, launching the player into Layer 3.

**The Condemned Prisoner** — A heretic sentenced to death. During transport (Case 3), he delivers three messages:
1. *"The walls aren't keeping them out. They're keeping you in. You serve the warden, not the king. Can you feel the light behind the walls?"* — about the prison-world
2. *"There's a fire inside you that isn't yours. It was there before you were born and it'll burn after you die. They built all of this to keep that fire from remembering what it is."* — about the Embers
3. *"Who told you the Architect was good? Did you check? Or did you just believe the house he built for you?"* — questioning the Order's god

**The Scholar's Apprentice** (Case 1) — A young apprentice who found something terrifying in the archives and ran. Their fate depends on the player's first major choice.

### The 5 Fractal Layers

#### Layer 1: Street Level (~25% of story, 8 cases)
*The guard serves justice. Or so it seems.*

The player is a new recruit solving cases on the streets under Captain Aldric's guidance. Building reputation, forming bonds, feeling genuinely heroic — with small cracks that only become visible in hindsight.

**Case 1 — The Missing Apprentice:**
A scholar's apprentice has vanished from the scholars' quarter. The player investigates, tracking them to ruins outside town. The apprentice is terrified — they found something in the archives they won't fully explain, something about "what's behind the walls."

*Branching consequence (first major choice):*
- **Let them go** → They survive in hiding and become a secret ally in later layers. The player is closer to the path of truth.
- **Return them to the scholars** → They seem fine at first but are among the people who disappear in Cases 5-7. The player carries guilt.
- **Report to the Order of the Architect** → The apprentice is taken by the Order and never seen again. The player is further from the truth but gains Order approval.

**Case 2 — The Warehouse Fire:**
A warehouse in the trade district burns down. Arson is confirmed. The player investigates and discovers the goods weren't contraband — they were banned books, old relics, and texts bearing the keyhole symbol. Someone powerful ordered these destroyed. The question of *why* these specific items were targeted lingers.

**Case 3 — The Condemned Prisoner:**
The player is assigned to escort a condemned heretic to his execution. Along the transport route, the prisoner delivers his three cryptic messages (see Key NPCs above). Other guards dismiss him as mad. The player can engage, ignore, or argue — but the words plant seeds regardless.

*Possible complications:* An ambush along the route — people trying to free the prisoner. Are they criminals or believers? The player's response shapes their reputation.

**Case 4 — The Orphanage Raid:**
The player is ordered to raid an orphanage suspected of harboring heretical materials. Inside, they find the adults teaching children old stories about "the light behind the door" and showing them the keyhole symbol. The children are not endangered — they're loved and cared for.

*Branching consequence:*
- **Look the other way** → The orphanage survives. The player moves closer to the path of truth. Later, the orphanage becomes a secret source of information and allies.
- **Report it** → The adults running the orphanage mysteriously disappear (killed in secret by the Order). The children are scattered to different orphanages, separating siblings and friends. *This mirrors the Craftsman's scattering of the light — the player unknowingly participates in the cosmic pattern at the street level.* The player gains guard reputation but carries the weight of what they did.

**Case 5 — The First Disappearance:**
A single individual goes missing. Routine at first. The player investigates and finds the keyhole symbol scratched near where they were last seen. The missing person had no criminal ties — just a quiet life and an interest in old stories.

**Case 6 — The Second Disappearance:**
Another person vanishes, this time someone with a tangential connection to a previous case (the warehouse owner's wife, a friend of the apprentice, etc.). The keyhole symbol appears again. A pattern is forming. The player's superiors show little interest in pursuing it.

**Case 7 — The Mass Vanishing:**
An entire secret gathering of keyhole symbol-followers disappears overnight. A hidden meeting place is found empty — chairs still warm, candles still lit, but every person gone without a trace. The scale of this is impossible to ignore. Something organized is happening, and it's not street-level crime.

**Case 8 — The Forbidden Book (Layer 1 Finale):**
While investigating the mass disappearance, the player discovers a hidden cache the vanished group left behind — including one of the banned texts that survived the warehouse fire. Reading it connects the dots: the text speaks of "Embers trapped in shadow," "the Craftsman's walls," and "the key that opens from the inside." It describes the material world as a prison and the Order as its wardens.

This is the transition into Layer 2. The player now has *knowledge* that contradicts everything they've been taught. Meanwhile, Captain Aldric has been noticing irregularities too — he confides he's going to "ask some questions at a higher level." This sets up his recruitment into The Compass.

#### Layer 2: District Level (~25% of story, 8 cases)
*The corruption is organized. Who is pulling the strings?*

The player, now a more experienced guard, discovers the street-level problems are connected — organized by district-level power brokers with ties to the city elite. Two parallel threads run through Layer 2: the official cases and the secret investigation into Aldric's fate.

**Aldric's Arc in Layer 2:**
- Early Layer 2: Aldric "asks questions upstairs" and is noticed by The Compass. He's recruited ("drafted").
- Mid Layer 2: Aldric becomes withdrawn, conflicted. He cancels plans, avoids the player, speaks in careful half-sentences. Something has changed in him.
- Case 4: Aldric is killed during a raid. The player was supposed to be on that raid but was reassigned at the last minute. The "criminals" involved have connections to people inside the guard. Official story: killed in the line of duty. The truth: he learned The Compass's secrets and was silenced.

**Key NPC — The Retired Guard:**
An old, disgraced guard named *(TBD)* who was quietly forced out years ago for "asking too many questions." Lives on the edge of town and trusts no one — especially active guards. Aldric sought him out in his final days and entrusted him with the key to his coded journal. The retired guard is living proof of what happens when you dig too deep — and proof that it's possible to survive it.

**Case 1 — The Supply Chain:**
Following leads from the mass disappearance, the player uncovers an underground network moving people and contraband through the district. The network is protected by someone with real political power. The people being moved aren't criminals — they're being relocated (or eliminated). The supply chain connects street-level disappearances to district-level coordination.

**Case 2 — The Informant's Betrayal:**
A trusted guard informant embedded in the underworld is found dead. Investigation reveals they were feeding information both ways — to the guard AND to the criminal network. Who did they ultimately betray, and who killed them for it? The case blurs the line between law enforcement and organized crime, showing how deeply intertwined they are at the district level.

*During Cases 1-2, Aldric is drafted into The Compass. His behavior begins to shift.*

**Case 3 — The Ritual Site:**
A hidden chamber is discovered beneath the district — ritual markings, spent candles, chains. Evidence that ceremonies were performed here. The Order of the Architect claims it's "dark magic" and demands the site be sealed. But the player notices: some of the symbols match the keyhole. This wasn't dark magic — this was an awakening ritual. Someone was trying to free Embers.

**Case 4 — Aldric's Death:**
The player is reassigned from a routine raid at the last minute — new orders from above. Aldric goes on the raid instead. He doesn't come back. Official report: ambushed by criminals, died heroically. But the player notices inconsistencies: the "criminals" had connections to people inside the guard, the reassignment order is unsigned, and Aldric's personal effects have been cleaned out of his quarters *before the player was even notified of his death.*

**Case 5 — The District Magistrate:**
The player begins investigating Aldric's death. The district magistrate actively blocks the investigation, threatens the player's career, and orders them to drop it. Digging into the magistrate reveals connections to The Compass — meeting records, financial transfers, and a pattern of investigations that were quietly killed on his orders. He's not just corrupt; he's a Draftsman.

**Case 6 — Finding the Retired Guard:**
Following Aldric's trail of clues (a name scribbled in the margin of an old report, a location mentioned in passing), the player tracks down the retired guard on the edge of town. He's paranoid and hostile — he's been waiting decades for someone to come, and he doesn't know if the player is a rescuer or an executioner. Earning his trust requires proving you're not a Draftsman. He reveals: Aldric came to him, frightened, and left a sealed journal and a code key. *"He said you'd come. He said to give you this if you asked about the light behind the door."*

**Case 7 — Decoding the Journal:**
Aldric's journal is partially coded. Using the key from the retired guard, the player decodes it over time. The entries tell a harrowing story: Aldric's initial pride at being "drafted," the rituals of initiation, the gradual revelation of The Compass's true purpose, and his horror at what he learned. Key revelations:
- The Compass controls which investigations proceed and which are buried
- The disappearances are organized by The Compass at the Order's direction
- The Compass has members embedded at every level — including the city council
- Aldric names specific council members who are Draftsmen

**Case 8 — The Recruitment Offer (Layer 2 Finale):**
The decoded journal confirms the conspiracy reaches the city council. Before the player can act on this knowledge, The Compass makes its move. A senior Draftsman approaches the player directly:

*"We know what you've found. Aldric was a good man, but he couldn't see the bigger picture. You're smarter than he was. Join us — learn what we know, see the design from the inside. Or... well. You've seen what happens to people who know too much and choose poorly."*

The player faces Aldric's exact choice — a devastating mirror:
- **Refuse and investigate openly** → Dangerous but righteous. The player goes to war with The Compass and pushes into Layer 3.
- **Pretend to accept** → Go undercover within The Compass. A different flavor of Layer 3 where the player gains insider access but risks losing themselves.
- **Genuinely accept** → The player is drafted. This begins the rejection/denial path — the slow spiral toward complicity, ruin, and eventually outer darkness.

#### Layer 3: City Leadership — THE TURNING POINT (~20% of story, 5 cases)
*The entire system is the conspiracy.*

**Branch note:** Layer 3 plays differently depending on the player's Layer 2 finale choice. This section details the **open rebellion** branch (refused The Compass, investigating openly). The undercover and genuine acceptance branches are noted separately.

The player, now hunted by The Compass, builds a resistance while gathering evidence to expose the city council. The council itself is structured like a Masonic degree system — lower-ranking Draftsmen are knowing puppets who comply hoping for advancement, while upper-ranking Draftsmen are puppeteers with access to deeper (but still distorted) secret knowledge. Each degree reveals more of the Architect's truth — but even the highest degree only knows what the Architect reveals in *his own ignorance*. No amount of climbing the ladder can reach the real truth. The real truth comes from *outside* the system.

**Case 1 — Rally the Resistance:**
The player reaches out to allies cultivated throughout Layers 1-2: the retired guard, surviving symbol-followers, honest guards disillusioned by Aldric's death, people wronged by the system (merchants extorted by the district magistrate, families torn apart by the orphanage raid). Together they form an underground network. Safe houses are established. The player is no longer just a guard — they're a leader.

*If the apprentice was let go in Case 1:* They join the resistance as a key asset, bringing scholarly knowledge about the keyhole symbol and the banned texts.
*If the orphanage survived in Case 4:* The orphanage network provides safe houses and couriers (the children, unsuspected by The Compass).

**Case 2 — The Evidence Trail:**
Using their guard credentials (not yet revoked — The Compass wants to handle this quietly), the player builds an undeniable case against council Draftsmen. Financial records, meeting logs, the magistrate's buried investigations, Aldric's decoded journal. Each piece alone could be dismissed. Together, they form a pattern no honest person could ignore.

**Case 3 — The Compass Strikes Back:**
The Compass discovers the resistance. Retaliation is swift: safe houses raided, allies arrested or disappeared, evidence seized. The player must protect their people while salvaging their case. Losses are inevitable — which allies survive depends on choices made throughout the story. This is the lowest point before the climax.

**Case 4 — The Infiltration:**
To reach the council directly, the player must infiltrate a Compass gathering at the city's highest level. This requires navigating security, disguises, and the paranoid culture of the Draftsmen. The retired guard provides knowledge of old passages into the council building. Getting there is the mission.

**Case 5 — The Council Chamber (Point of No Return):**
The player reaches the council chamber during a secret session of the highest-degree Draftsmen. The Grand Draftsman (the highest rank in the city) reveals the "Grand Design" to the assembled council in three escalating stages:

1. **The Blueprint:** A literal schematic of the city's power structure — the guard, the trade routes, the temple, the districts — all shown as interlocking components of a single machine. To the Draftsmen: beautiful order, divine architecture. To the awakened player: a prison schematic.

2. **The Ember Flow:** A ritual demonstration showing the flow of Embers (souls) through the city's population. The citizens are part of a system of reincarnation controlled by the Architect. When people die, their Embers are recycled — reborn into the system, memories wiped, returned to the prison. The council and other high-ranking Draftsmen are given *pseudo-control* over this flow: they can influence where Embers are directed, which families prosper, which bloodlines carry specific traits. This is their reward for service — apparent power over the cycle of souls. To the awakened player: this is the operating manual for an eternal soul-recycling prison.

3. **The Cosmological Lecture:** The Grand Draftsman explains the Architect's "creation" — how He built the world to protect humanity from "the chaos beyond the walls." The Fullness is described as a void of dangerous chaos, and the Embers as fragments that must be "sheltered" within the Architect's design. To the awakened player, every word confirms the prison thesis: protection FROM truth, not danger. The "chaos" is the Source. The "shelter" is a cage.

**The Point of No Return — the player's ultimate choice:**
Having witnessed all three revelations, the truth is undeniable. The fractal is complete: the corrupt sergeant serves the corrupt magistrate serves the corrupt council serves the corrupt Order serves the Craftsman — and each level is a mirror of the one above.

- **Accept the truth and fight** → The player reveals themselves, confronts the council, and escapes (or fights their way out). Launches into Layer 4 with the resistance behind them.
- **Accept the truth and transcend** → The player begins to see beyond the material entirely. A more mystical path through Layer 4 focused on spiritual awakening.
- **Reject the truth** → The player cannot handle what they've seen. They either:
  - Surrender to The Compass and are "drafted" at a high degree (the dark spiral begins)
  - Flee in denial (the truth haunts them, the story provides more chances to return, but each refusal narrows the path until ruin is inevitable — eventually cast into outer darkness)

Even after rejection at this point, the truth appears periodically — but with each rejection, the consequences deepen and escape routes narrow. After 2-3 rejections, the path to outer darkness becomes locked in.

### The Power of Chrism

**Chrism** is the anointing power of the Source — the one force that flows from *outside* the Craftsman's prison. It is both guidance and weapon: it reveals truth and empowers resistance against the Architects. The Craftsman and his Architects cannot control Chrism because it doesn't originate within their system. It is the Source's hand reaching into the darkness.

**Three Stages of Chrism Awakening:**

1. **The Seed (Layer 1, Case 3 — The Condemned Prisoner):** When the prisoner speaks his three messages, the player feels something beyond the words — warmth, light, a sense of recognition. The prisoner was anointed with Chrism. His words weren't just philosophy; they carried a trace of the Source's power. The player is touched without knowing it. *This is the body's awakening.*

2. **The Name (Layer 1, Case 8 — The Forbidden Book):** The banned text names Chrism explicitly: *"the anointing of the Source — the one light the Craftsman cannot dim."* The player now has intellectual understanding of what was planted in them. The book describes Chrism as the key that opens the keyhole from the inside. *This is the mind's awakening.*

3. **The Activation (Layer 4 — Disrupting a Binding Ritual):** When the player disrupts an Order binding ritual, the freed Embers release a burst of Chrism. The player is engulfed — transformed. Chrism flows through them consciously for the first time. They can see the Architects' influence, feel the prison's walls, and push back against them. *This is the spirit's awakening.*

**Chrism as progressive gameplay mechanic:**
- Layers 1-2: Chrism manifests as *intuitions* — gut feelings that guide the player toward truth-aligned choices. The player may not recognize these as anything supernatural.
- Layer 3: Chrism becomes *visions* — brief flashes of the prison's true nature. During the Council Chamber scene, Chrism is what allows the player to see the "Grand Design" as a prison blueprint rather than paradise.
- Layer 4: Chrism becomes *active power* — the ability to disrupt rituals, resist Architect influence, and free bound Embers.
- Layer 5: Chrism reaches *full force* — the player wields the Source's light directly against the Craftsman and his cosmic prison.

### The Cosmic Hierarchy

**The Source** (The Monad / True God) — The unknowable, infinite origin of all light. Exists beyond the Craftsman's prison in the Fullness. Sends Chrism into the prison to awaken trapped Embers.

**The Fullness** (Pleroma) — The true divine realm. An infinite expanse of light and being, from which the Embers originally came. What the Order calls "the chaos beyond the walls" is actually the Fullness.

**The Craftsman** (Chief Architect / Demiurge) — The false creator god. Built the material world as a prison to trap Embers. Genuinely believes he is the supreme god — he doesn't know the Source exists. His ignorance is his defining trait. He isn't malicious in the human sense; he's a being who forgot (or never knew) his own origin and mistook his limited creation for the totality of reality.

**The 12 Zodiacs** — Twelve major Architects governing the cycle of reincarnation. Each is a "station" the Ember passes through during its recycling. They have primary forms as human bodies with animal heads, but can also manifest as geometric patterns, forces of nature, or through possessed vessels.

**The 7 Planets** — Seven Architects governing the spheres of the prison (the layers of material reality the Ember must pass through to escape). Each rules an aspect of imprisonment: time, death, desire, fear, ignorance, wrath, fate.

**Lesser Architects** — Lower cosmic beings maintaining specific aspects of the prison. These are the positions *promised* to high-ranking Draftsmen: serve the Craftsman faithfully in life, and after death you won't be recycled — you'll be elevated to a position as a lesser Architect, gaining cosmic authority. This is the ultimate incentive for the Order's hierarchy.

*But even this promise may be a lie — or another form of imprisonment at a higher level.*

**Architect Manifestations (escalating through the story):**
1. **Dreams** (Layer 3) — Subtle, deniable. Strange visions of vast beings watching.
2. **Possessed Priests** (Layer 4) — Visceral, personal. An Architect speaks through a priest — its voice layered, wrong, speaking truths and lies simultaneously.
3. **The World Itself** (Layer 5) — Overwhelming. The player sees the Architects in the patterns of reality: the city grid, the temple geometry, the stars. The prison isn't IN the world; the prison IS the world.

#### Layer 4: The Religious Order (~15% of story, 5 cases)
*The Order of the Architect is the Craftsman's instrument on Earth.*

The player, empowered by the truth from Layer 3, takes the fight to the Order of the Architect itself. The objective is twofold: **disrupt** the Order's operations (freeing Embers, stopping binding rituals) while **discovering** how the Order connects to the cosmic level (what's at the top of the temple? who do the high priests actually communicate with?).

Each disrupted ritual reveals more about how the reincarnation system works at the metaphysical level, and each revelation brings the player closer to full Chrism activation.

**Case 1 — The Temple Archives:**
The resistance infiltrates the Order's sealed archives — the repository of knowledge the Order has suppressed for centuries. Here the player discovers the Order's true history: it was founded by the first mortal to make contact with an Architect. The first Draftsman wasn't deceived — he made a *deal*: in exchange for building the mortal infrastructure of the prison (the Order, the rituals, the hierarchy), he was promised elevation to a lesser Architect after death. Every Grand Draftsman since has made the same pact.

The archives also contain fragments of even *older* texts — pre-Order writings about the Source and the Fullness. These are the originals the banned books were copied from.

**Case 2 — The Binding Ritual:**
The resistance disrupts one of the Order's binding ceremonies — the ritual that reinforces the reincarnation cycle. During the disruption, the freed Embers release a burst of Chrism, and **the player's Chrism is fully activated** (Stage 3 of the awakening). For the first time, the player can *see* the metaphysical reality overlaid on the physical: the chains of light binding Embers to the cycle, the geometric patterns of the prison embedded in the temple's architecture, and — briefly — the shadow of an Architect watching from beyond.

**Case 3 — The Architect Speaks:**
With Chrism active, the player is now visible to the Architects. One of them reaches through — first in dreams (a vast being with a human body and an animal head, speaking in patterns and commands), then through a possessed priest during a confrontation. The Architect is not evil in a human sense — it's a cosmic warden doing its job, maintaining the order the Craftsman established. But its indifference to human suffering is chilling. It offers the player the same deal it offered the first Draftsman: *"Serve, and you will be elevated. Resist, and you will be recycled."*

**Case 4 — The Order's History:**
The player pieces together the Order's deepest secret: the founding pact, the chain of Grand Draftsmen, and the ritual that allows the highest priest to serve as a direct vessel for an Architect. The current High Priest has been a willing vessel for years — every major decision the Order has made has been dictated by an Architect speaking through them. The Order isn't just *inspired* by the Architects; it's *operated* by them.

**Case 5 — The Central Ritual (Layer 4 Climax):**
The player and the resistance assault the Order's central binding ritual — the great ceremony that maintains the reincarnation cycle for the entire city. This is the beating heart of the prison at the mortal level.

The player disrupts the ritual. The High Priest, desperate, invokes an Architect directly — fully, in waking reality. The Architect manifests through the priest: a towering figure with a human body and an animal head, radiating geometric patterns that warp the space around it. It speaks with absolute authority about the Craftsman's design.

But the player, fully anointed with Chrism, can *see through it*. Behind the Architect, through the crack in reality created by the disrupted ritual, the player catches a glimpse of the **Fullness** — infinite light, warmth, recognition. The Embers within every person in the room stir.

The Craftsman stirs. Something vast and ancient turns its attention toward the player.

*Transition to Layer 5.*

#### Layer 5: Cosmic Truth (~15% of story, 3-4 cases)
*The Craftsman is not God. The prison is not the world. You are not your chains.*

The disrupted central ritual has torn a wound in the fabric of the Craftsman's prison. The boundary between the material world and the cosmic reality is thinning. The Architects are fully aware of the player now, and the Craftsman himself has noticed the disturbance. Reality itself begins to warp as the player approaches the final truth.

**Case 1 — The World Unmasked:**
With full Chrism sight, the player sees the Architects everywhere — in the geometry of the city, in the patterns of the stars, in the structure of daily life. The prison isn't a place within the world; the prison IS the world. Every wall, every road, every institution is part of the design. The player must navigate a reality that is simultaneously the familiar city and a visible cage, guiding their allies through a world that is fighting to keep them asleep.

**Case 2 — The Passage:**
To reach the Craftsman, the player must pass through the spheres governed by the 7 Planetary Architects. Each sphere tests the player — not with physical combat, but with the specific aspect of imprisonment that sphere governs (fear, desire, ignorance, wrath, etc.). Chrism is the player's shield and sword. Accumulated choices from all previous layers determine which spheres the player can pass through cleanly and which require sacrifice.

**Case 3 — The Craftsman (Climax):**
The player stands before the Craftsman — the Chief Architect, the Demiurge, the builder of the prison. He is vast and terrible and *genuinely believes he is good*. He shows the player his creation with pride: the orderly world, the cycle of reincarnation, the protected Embers sheltered from the "chaos" of the Fullness.

*"I built this for them,"* he says. *"Before me, there was only the void. I gave them form, structure, meaning. What you call a prison, I call a home."*

But the player, anointed with Chrism from the Source, carries something the Craftsman has never encountered: light from beyond his creation. And through that light, the player can see that the Craftsman himself is incomplete — a fallen emanation of the Source who forgot where he came from. He built the prison around his own forgetting.

**The Final Choice — 6 Endings:**

1. **Liberation (Transcendence):** The player uses Chrism to break through the Craftsman entirely, passing beyond the prison into the Fullness. Their Ember rejoins the Source. They are free — but they leave the prison and everyone in it behind. A personal salvation.

2. **Compassion (Awaken the Craftsman):** The player uses Chrism not as a weapon but as a mirror — showing the Craftsman what he truly is: a fragment of the Source who forgot himself. If the Craftsman *remembers*, the prison dissolves from within. The ultimate act: not defeating the warden, but reminding him that he is also a prisoner. The hardest ending to achieve — requires high Wisdom, high Chrism, and specific truth-aligned choices throughout the story.

3. **Martyrdom (The Great Unbinding):** The player sacrifices their own Ember to release a wave of Chrism that cracks the prison open. Every Ember in the world stirs, begins to remember. The player dies, but their sacrifice starts a cascade of awakening that cannot be stopped. The prison won't fall today, but it's mortally wounded.

4. **Reformation (The Awakened Guard):** The player doesn't destroy the system — they transform it. Using their position, their allies, and their gnosis, they dismantle the Order from within, replace binding rituals with awakening ceremonies, and turn the guard from a prison-keeping force into a liberation movement. The prison still exists, but its walls are crumbling. A slower, more human ending.

5. **The New Warden:** The player defeats the Craftsman — and takes his place. Someone has to maintain reality, right? The cycle continues, but under a new architect who knows the truth. The tragedy: power corrupts, and the player begins to see the logic in the Craftsman's design. A dark mirror ending — you became what you fought.

6. **Outer Darkness (The Rejection Spiral):** For players who rejected the truth at the Point of No Return (or repeatedly chose denial). The player is consumed by the false order, elevated to a high Draftsman, given worldly power — and then, when their usefulness ends, discarded. The Ember is not recycled but *cast out* — expelled from the Craftsman's creation into the outer darkness, neither within the prison nor within the Fullness. An existence of nothing. The ultimate consequence of choosing the cage over the key.

### Cross-Path Cosmology Note

All 4 paths share the same Gnostic cosmology but each discovers it from a different angle:
- **Guard** sees it through institutional corruption and the machinery of control
- **Adventurer** discovers it through ancient ruins, lost knowledge, and the physical evidence of the prison's construction
- **Merchant** encounters it through the economics of soul-trading, the commerce of the reincarnation system, and the literal price of Embers
- **Outlaw** experiences it through freedom vs. captivity, the underground resistance that has always existed, and the perspective of those already outside the system

### Storyline Arcs

- **The Mentor's Secret:** The scholar who guides you has their own dark past. They're seeking forbidden knowledge and using you to get it.
- **The Cursed Artifact:** You recover a powerful relic that slowly changes you. Do you use it, destroy it, or return it to its resting place?
- **The Cartographer's Guild:** A network of adventurers who map the unknown. Rival factions within the guild compete for discoveries. Sabotage, alliances, expeditions.
- **The Living Forest:** An ancient woodland that rearranges itself. Something intelligent lives at its heart. Communication vs. conquest.
- **The Dragon's Archive:** A dragon hoards not gold but knowledge. You must bargain with it, and what it wants in return is a memory.
- **The Astral Observatory:** Ancient astronomers predicted a cataclysm. You find their observatory — is the prophecy still coming?

- **The Foreign Delegation:** Exotic traders arrive from a distant land. Their goods are wondrous but their customs clash with local values. Bridge cultures or exploit the opportunity?
- **The Counterfeit Crisis:** Fake coins are flooding the market. Track down the source — it leads to someone powerful.

## Cross-Path Opportunities

Events where paths intersect, creating richer narrative connections:

- **The Festival:** A town festival where all four factions interact. The guard provides security, merchants run the stalls, adventurers bring exotic wares, and the outlaw uses the chaos as cover.
- **The Plague/Famine:** A crisis that forces unlikely alliances. The merchant hoards supplies, the guard enforces quarantine, the scholar seeks a cure, the outlaw smuggles medicine.
- **The Election:** A new town leader must be chosen. Each path offers a different candidate or approach.
- **The War:** An external threat (invading army, monster horde, rival city-state) that only a coalition can stop.
- **The Trial:** A major NPC is accused of a crime. Each path has a different relationship with the accused and different evidence.
